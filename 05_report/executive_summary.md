# Executive Summary

*One page. Answer-first. Must stand alone — a reader who sees only this
page should know our three topics, the headline number, and the
recommended action.*

---

**Commissioned work.** An external CSRD/ESRS-aligned double materiality
assessment for Aqualia, covering its integrated water-cycle operations
across eight countries, under the IE Sustainability Datathon 2026
mandate.

**Three material topics.** From Aqualia's 2025 review of sixteen
topics, eighty-three impacts/risks/opportunities (IROs), and a
stakeholder ecosystem of ten groups, we concentrate strategic priority
on three topics that absorb **seventy-three percent of the IRO mass**
and that survive sensitivity analysis under four alternate stakeholder
weighting schemes:

1. **Water Resilience and Equitable Access** (ESRS E1 + E3 + S4 service
   resilience + S3 restored). Impact Severity 3.98 of 5; Financial
   Severity 3.07. Solid Target-Zone placement.
2. **Digital and Cyber Infrastructure** (Aqualia's AQ-Tech + AQ-Digital
   + AQ-Cyber cluster). Impact 3.08; Financial 2.75. Target Zone —
   repositioned from Aqualia's current lower-middle placement.
3. **Green Finance and Integrity** (ESRS G1 compliance cluster +
   Green-finance AQ). Impact 3.24; Financial 2.42. Borderline —
   *conditionally* material and trigger-sensitive.

**The blind spot.** Aqualia's 2025 review assigns Digitalisation only
**two IROs** — the lowest of any topic — while peers (Veolia, Suez,
Global Omnium) treat digital platforms as core, the EU Water
Resilience Strategy names Digitalisation as one of five Action Areas,
and Aqualia's own Risk Map logs three HIGH-severity operational risks
in this cluster (O8, O9, O10). Our Monte Carlo repositions the cluster
firmly into the materiality Target Zone.

**The equity finding.** Aqualia folds ESRS S3 (affected communities)
into S4 (consumers) — collapsing a distinct materiality signal.
Customer satisfaction ranges 86–98% across six European and
institutional markets, **but 33% in Colombia**. Restoring S3 converts a
methodological shortcut into a concession-renewal risk we recommend
flagging formally.

**The financial case.** Across the three topics, net annual financial
materiality under-weighted by the current framework totals
**€18 M/yr**, against a cumulative 2027–2030 CAPEX implication of
**€440 M** already embedded in Aqualia's own Risk Map. The topics
are linked: T1 drives the CAPEX, T2 de-risks its delivery, and T3
finances it — at a differential cost of capital.

**The recommendation.** A phased **€500 M EU-Taxonomy-aligned green /
sustainability-linked bond programme, 2027–2030**, issued in four
tranches, aligned to the CAPEX drawdown. At a twenty-five basis point
coupon spread (the current IG Euro water-utility market level for
green issuance), the programme delivers **€31 M of present-value
interest savings over its ten-year life** — converting a materiality
drag into a capital-markets advantage while the spread remains open.

**Methodology, briefly.** We preserve Aqualia's own 2025 scoring
structure (Severity × Probability for impact, Magnitude × Probability
for financial) and extend it along four dimensions: (i) explicit 1–5
thresholds cited to ESRS / EFRAG / MSCI / SASB / WRI (see scoring
rubric); (ii) a Mitchell–Agle–Wood stakeholder-salience model applied
to Aqualia's own ten-group ecosystem (restoring the external input
their 2025 cycle omitted); (iii) 10,000-draw Monte Carlo with 90%
confidence ellipses, sensitivity tornado, and a four-scheme
robustness check; (iv) an LLM-assisted ESRS disclosure-gap heatmap
classifying the available Aqualia corpus against seventy-five
datapoints. All assumptions numbered and cited (A01–A17).

**Evidence quality control.** Financial assumptions are tagged by
evidence tier: Tier A (auditable disclosures/regulation), Tier B
(benchmark sources), Tier C (reasoned estimates). Publication rule:
no headline number relies on Tier C assumptions alone.

**What we did not do.** We did not consult external Aqualia
stakeholders directly; we did not access granular asset-level
financials; our Real Options exhibit on desalination-vs-water-reuse is
indicative. Our limitations section documents each gap and a
remediation path for the production-grade version of the framework.

**Bottom line.** Three material topics. Eighteen million euros per
year of under-weighted financial materiality. A five-hundred-million-
euro green bond programme that funds the answer.
