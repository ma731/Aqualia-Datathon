## 1.  T1 — Water Resilience & Equitable Access

### 1.1  What it bundles

| Aqualia 2025 topic | ESRS | IROs absorbed |
|---|---|---|
| Mitigation & adaptation to climate change | E1 | 9 |
| Sustainability of the water resource | E3 | 9 |
| Service resilience and security | S4 | 8 |
| *(newly surfaced — our restoration of S3)* | S3 | cross-cut |
| **Total** | **E1+E3+S3+S4** | **26 + cross-cut** |

Covers ~31% of all 83 IROs in Aqualia's review — the single most
material cluster in their taxonomy, and the one judges cannot argue
against.

### 1.2  Why it's material

- **Impact side.** WEF 2025–2026 ranks extreme weather as #4 short-term
  and #1 long-term global risk; five of the top-10 long-term risks
  are water-related; water demand will exceed supply by **40% by
  2030** (source: WEF / UN Water). Aqualia's `O5` risk ("inadequate
  climate adaptation — agricultural + water stress") is HIGH-severity
  by their own scoring.
- **Financial side.** Directive 2024/3019 (recast UWWTD) mandates
  energy-neutral WWTP and circular economy — direct CAPEX driver.
  EU "Water Efficiency First" plans target **−10% demand by 2030**.
  Royal Decree 1985/2024 opens reuse market = revenue upside.
  Aqualia opportunities `OpO1`, `OpO2`, `OpO3` all sit here.
- **Stakeholder side.** Under our salience model, the top-salience
  stakeholders (Customers 0.120, Regulators 0.120, Local
  communities 0.080, Environment 0.080 → 40% combined) converge on
  this topic.

### 1.3  Pitch-ready finding embedded in T1

> **Colombia customer satisfaction is 33% vs 88–94% in Europe.**
> Aqualia folded ESRS S3 (affected communities) into S4 — a
> methodological shortcut that buries a concrete equity-access
> issue. Restoring S3 strengthens impact materiality AND captures
> concession-renewal risk at the financial layer.

### 1.4  ESRS datapoint hit-list (to map in the gap heatmap)

Core DRs: **E1-1, E1-4, E1-5, E1-6, E3-1, E3-2, E3-3, E3-4, E3-5,
S3-1, S3-3, S3-4, S4-1, S4-2, S4-3, S4-4**. Climate-scenario analysis
(E1-IRO-1) is a likely gap.

### 1.5  "Me too" or real advantage?
- **Me too component:** E1/E3/S4 — unavoidable. Every peer has them.
- **Real advantage:** bundling with S3 (equity) + naming the Colombia
  outlier converts a defensive topic into an actionable concession
  portfolio review.

## 2.  T2 — Digital & Cyber Infrastructure

### 2.1  What it bundles

| Aqualia 2025 topic | ESRS | IROs absorbed |
|---|---|---|
| Technological & infrastructure innovation *(AQ)* | G1 | 5 |
| Digitalisation *(AQ)* | S4 | 2 |
| Commitment to cybersecurity *(AQ)* | G1 | 4 |
| **Total** | **AQ cluster** | **11** |

### 2.2  Why it's material — THE HERO FINDING

- **Under-weighted in Aqualia's taxonomy.** Only 11 IROs cover the
  three AQ-labelled digital/innovation/cyber topics. Digitalisation
  alone has **2 IROs — the lowest of any topic in the 2025 matrix**.
- **Over-weighted in the real world.**
  - **EU Water Resilience Strategy:** Digitalisation is Action Area 3
    of 5 — a first-order pillar.
  - **WEF 2025–2026:** Cyber insecurity is #6 short-term global risk
    and #8 long-term.
  - **Peer benchmark:** Veolia (AquaCIS), Suez (Aquadvanced),
    Global Omnium (GoAigua) all treat digital platforms as core.
  - **MSCI 2025:** Cybersecurity is a named emerging material risk
    for water utilities.
- **Operational risk ownership.** Aqualia's own Risk Map catalogues:
  `O8` (Poor deployment of digital solutions, HIGH),
  `O9` (Deficient cyberattack response plan, HIGH),
  `O10` (Deficiencies in customer management, MEDIUM/GDPR).
  These are HIGH risks sitting under a 2-IRO topic.

### 2.3  Pitch-ready finding embedded in T2

> **Digitalisation is Aqualia's blind spot.** Only 2 IROs were logged
> in the 2025 review, yet peers treat it as a platform, the EU names
> it a pillar, and three of their own HIGH operational risks sit here.
> Our Monte Carlo repositions this topic from the lower-middle of
> their matrix into the top-right Target Zone.

### 2.4  ESRS datapoint hit-list

Partly implicit in G1 (cyber resilience, data governance) + emerging
**ESRS G1-7 / G1-8** on business conduct governance + **ESRS S4-2**
(data privacy for consumers). The **EU Cybersecurity Act + NIS2
Directive** overlay is likely a disclosure gap.

### 2.5  Me too or real advantage?
- **Me too component:** digital platforms themselves — peers each have
  one.
- **Real advantage:** *naming cybersecurity as a first-order FINANCIAL
  materiality* (not just an operational compliance issue) is
  differentiated. €-quantification (see `financials.md`) transforms
  it from IT line-item to boardroom topic.

## 3.  T3 — Green Finance & Integrity

### 3.1  What it bundles

| Aqualia 2025 topic | ESRS | IROs absorbed |
|---|---|---|
| Culture of legal, contractual & social compliance | G1 | 11 |
| Fight against corruption | G1 | 3 |
| Financial management & green finance *(AQ)* | G1 | 6 |
| Commitment to labour rights & HR in supply chain | G1 | 4 |
| **Total** | **G1 cluster** | **24** |

The highest-IRO cluster in the entire 2025 review (24 IROs — more
than any individual topic).

### 3.2  Why it's material

- **Regulatory gravity.** CSRD + CSDDD + Omnibus I Simplification +
  EU Taxonomy + Spanish Ley 7/2021 + Clean Industrial Deal + Nature
  Credits Roadmap — the compliance surface area is expanding, not
  contracting, and every gap costs financing options.
- **Financial upside.** Aqualia's own 2025 risk inventory names THREE
  NEW financial risks (`F1` loss of financing capacity, `F2` FX,
  `F3` interest rates) — all CFO-owned. Opportunities `OpF1`, `OpF2`,
  `OpF3`, `OpS1` are concentrated here.
- **Cost of capital mechanism.** The spread between ESG-rating leaders
  and laggards in utilities is measurable (Refinitiv, MSCI) —
  typically 15–40 bps on long-dated issuance. For Aqualia's CAPEX
  pipeline this is real money (see `financials.md` T3 section).

### 3.3  Pitch-ready finding embedded in T3

> **Aqualia is ahead of peers on promoting green finance to
> stand-alone material status.** This is a genuine differentiator we
> can amplify: our 2027–2030 roadmap proposes a EU-Taxonomy-aligned
> **€500M green bond programme** — a specific, defensible, costed
> strategic move that no peer has publicly committed to.

### 3.4  ESRS datapoint hit-list

**G1-1, G1-2, G1-3, G1-5, G1-6** core; **E1-3 (transition plan
financing)** for taxonomy alignment; **Art. 8 Taxonomy Regulation**
KPIs (eligibility + alignment); **SFDR PAI** indicators if Aqualia
sells into ESG-labelled funds.

### 3.5  Me too or real advantage?
- **Me too component:** compliance culture — every peer has it.
- **Real advantage:** promoting **green finance** as its own material
  topic before peers; we amplify with a specific green bond ask that
  reframes sustainability from cost to capital-markets advantage.
