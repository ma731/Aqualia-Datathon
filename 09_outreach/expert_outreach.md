# Expert Outreach — LinkedIn + Email Templates

*Goal: one 15-minute call with a water-utility sustainability lead. One
citable line moves the report from "student" to "consulting." The
outreach lag (4–7 days) is the single bottleneck — start day 1.*

---

## 0. Targeting — who to approach, in priority order

| Tier | Role | Example companies | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Head of Sustainability / ESG Director | **Severn Trent** (UK, CSRD-adjacent under SDR), **Veolia Iberia**, **Suez** | Most direct analogue; published CSRD / sustainability reports give you conversation hooks |
| **A** | Double Materiality / CSRD Lead | ERM, South Pole, EcoAct (BCG-owned), PwC Sustainability, EY Climate Change & Sustainability | Consultants who do this work professionally — often more responsive than in-house |
| **B** | Water resilience / Climate adaptation specialist | WRI, WWF Water, European Environment Agency | Subject-matter expertise; less sensitive to competitive dynamics |
| **B** | IE Faculty / Alumni in ESG consulting | IE Center for Sustainability faculty, alumni network | Warm intro via the programme; often fast turnaround |
| **C** | Aqualia directly (LAST RESORT) | Aqualia Sustainability & Innovation Department | Use only if upstream outreach fails; risks looking like you skipped peer research |

**Do not approach:** current Aqualia executives by name, competitors' M&A / finance teams, board members.

**Target count:** 6–10 messages sent across Tiers A + B. Realistic
response rate 10–20% → expect 1–2 conversations.

## 3. The 15-minute call agenda

When they say yes — run a tight call. Time block:

| Minutes | Topic |
|---|---|
| 0–1 | Warm intro: who we are, one-sentence thesis, what we need |
| 1–4 | Share the three short-listed topics + the hero finding |
| 4–8 | Ask the one pre-sent question most relevant to their expertise |
| 8–12 | Follow-up probe: concrete data point we can cite |
| 12–14 | Ask: "If you were reviewing this for Aqualia's board, what would you challenge hardest?" |
| 14–15 | Thank them; confirm attribution preference; promise to send final report |

**Rules:**
- Record (with their permission) or take notes with a partner.
- Do not ask them to review the deck live — that's a different ask.
- If they volunteer a quote, **write it down verbatim** and read it
  back at the end of the call to confirm.
- Send a thank-you within 24 hours with a one-paragraph summary of
  what you heard — gives them the chance to correct before it
  appears in the report.

---

## 4. The deliverable from one successful call

A single paragraph in the report (Appendix F) + a direct quote that
you reference in §9 Limitations. Example format:

> *"[One quoted sentence from the expert]."* — [Name], [Role],
> [Organisation], [Date of call]. [Optional second sentence giving
> context or a specific data point they confirmed.]

That single citation converts your report from a well-researched
student exercise to a consulting deliverable.

## 6. Escape valves — if no external expert responds

If the outreach round yields zero calls, the fall-back is legitimate
and citable:

1. **Published expert commentary** — pull one quote from a recent
   interview with a water-sector sustainability lead (Reuters,
   Financial Times, Global Water Intel). Cite with a footnote
   pointing to the source article.
2. **Peer disclosure as proxy expert voice** — a named quote from
   Veolia's or Suez's 2024 Universal Registration Document attributed
   to the Chief Sustainability Officer can substitute for a private
   call.
3. **Regulatory authority** — one paragraph from the EU Water
   Resilience Strategy or an EFRAG IG-1 paragraph cited verbatim.

These are weaker than a private call but still keep the report from
looking inward-facing. Use as a floor, not a target.
