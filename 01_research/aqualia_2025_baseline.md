## 0.  Executive summary (for internal team)

- Aqualia has **already completed** a 2025 comprehensive Double Materiality
  Review. It produced **16 material topics**, **31 material impacts**
  (16 negative + 15 positive), **24 material risks**, and
  **11 material opportunities** — all mapped to **ESRS topical standards**
  and to Aqualia's **Strategic Sustainability Plan (PESA)** 2024–2026.
- Our job is NOT to reinvent this — it's to **independently assess**
  which **three** of those 16 topics are truly strategic for 2027–2030, and
  to prove it with a more rigorous methodology (Monte Carlo, stakeholder
  salience, € quantification, scenario overlay) than they applied
  themselves.
- **Competitive edge hint**: Aqualia's own 2025 methodology is qualitative
  (scale/scope/remediability rated 1–4 by Sustainability Dept.
  internally, no external stakeholder consultation this cycle). That is a
  gap we can exploit. They did NOT quantify € magnitude externally; we can.
- Aqualia explicitly benchmarks against **Veolia Water, Suez, Saur, Facsa,
  Global Omnium**. Our peer set should match or exceed this.

## 4.  Aqualia's scoring methodology (match, then extend)

Source: `Methodology Double Materiality Assessment Review.pdf`.

### 4.1  Impact materiality — formulas

- **Severity** = (Scale + Scope + Remediability) / 3   *(each on 1–4)*
- **Current impact** consolidated score = **Severity × 4**
  *(probability fixed at max since it is already occurring)*
- **Potential impact** consolidated score = **Severity × Probability**

### 4.2  Probability scale (1–4)

| Score | Label | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Unlikely | <10% in next 5 years |
| 2 | Possible | 10–30% in next 5 years |
| 3 | Likely | 30–70% in next 5 years |
| 4 | Almost certain | >70% in next 3 years |

### 4.3  Time horizon

| Label | Duration |
|---|---|
| Short | ≤ 3 years |
| Medium | 3–10 years |
| Long | > 10 years |

### 4.4  Financial materiality — formula

- **Severity of R&O** = **Average Magnitude × Probability**
- Magnitude assessed per affected asset (magnitude tables in their
  internal Excel `VF_DMA_Proceso&Analisis`).
- Three-step validation: Sustainability Dept → Strategic Dev &
  Management System → Executive Committee.

### 4.5  Our methodology delta (how we outclass)

| Element | Aqualia 2025 | Our approach |
|---|---|---|
| Severity inputs | 3 factors, 1–4 scale, internally rated | Same 3 factors but **1–5 scale** with cited thresholds + **Monte Carlo** over uncertain inputs (triangular distributions) |
| Stakeholder consultation | **None this cycle (internal only)** | **Mitchell–Agle–Wood salience model** applied to their stakeholder map |
| Probability | Qualitative 1–4 | Same 1–4 bands but with **frequency-of-occurrence literature citations** per band |
| Financial magnitude | "Magnitude tables" (undisclosed) | **Explicit € ranges** (low/base/high) with sources; **Real Options** on one CAPEX decision |
| Matrix output | Presumed point plot | **90% confidence ellipses** from MC; **tornado chart** of sensitivity |
| Scenario analysis | Mentioned (TNFD + climate) but not published | **NGFS Orderly / Disorderly / Hot House** overlay + **WRI Aqueduct 4.0** map |
| ESRS compliance view | Topic-level mapping | **Datapoint-level ESRS gap heatmap** (LLM-powered) |

## 8.  Working hypotheses for our 3 material topics

Early read (to be stress-tested against scoring):

### H1 — "Water Resource Resilience" (E3 + E1 + S4 Service Resilience)
- **Why:** Bundles the highest-IRO topics (E3=9, E1=9, S4=8 = 26 IROs).
  Directly aligned to EU Water Resilience Strategy. WEF flags water
  demand/supply gap of 40% by 2030. Aqualia's **Colombia 33%
  satisfaction** provides a concrete inequity case.
- **Financial handle:** OpF3 (nature credits) + OpO2 (alternative
  resources) + O5 (climate adaptation risk). Real Options on
  **desalination vs reuse** infrastructure.
- **Scenario anchor:** NGFS + WRI Aqueduct 4.0 overlay on Spain service area.

### H2 — "Infrastructure & Cyber Resilience" (Tech innov. + Digital + Cyber)
- **Why:** Three topics with collectively only 11 IROs but cutting across
  **EU Action Areas 3 + 5**, **MSCI emerging cyber risk**, and **O8, O9,
  OpO5**. Digitalisation is *under-weighted* by Aqualia (2 IROs) — our
  blind-spot finding.
- **Financial handle:** OpF1 (innovation-led financing), OpF2 (green
  finance), O9 (cyberattack response plan deficiency). Quantifiable
  via sector cyber-incident cost data.
- **Roadmap anchor:** digital twin + sectorisation + predictive pipe
  renewal.

### H3 — "Governance Integrity & Green Finance" (G1 compliance + Green finance)
- **Why:** Compliance has **11 IROs** — the highest density of any
  topic. F1–F3 under finance are all **NEW risks in 2025**. OpS1, OpF2,
  OpF3 are the main **upside drivers**.
- **Financial handle:** Cost of capital spread between ESG leaders vs
  laggards (Refinitiv / MSCI data). €-quantified via green bond coupon
  differential.
- **Regulatory anchor:** CSDDD, CSRD/Omnibus I, ISO 14001:2026, Royal
  Decree 1985/2024, EU Taxonomy alignment.

> **Note on H3**: "Compliance" alone is too defensive for a McKinsey
> pitch. Pair it with **Green Finance** as the upside to create a
> balanced topic. If our matrix says H3 is too broad, we split it.
