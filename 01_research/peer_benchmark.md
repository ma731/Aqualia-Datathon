## 3.  Aqualia blind spots vs peers (our "findings")

### Finding 1 — Digitalisation under-weighted
- Aqualia flags Digitalisation (S4) with only **2 IROs** — lowest of
  any topic in their 2025 review.
- **Veolia, Suez, and Global Omnium** each treat digitalisation /
  digital twin / AI platforms as **core** (Veolia AquaCIS; Suez
  Aquadvanced; Global Omnium GoAigua).
- This contradicts **EU Water Resilience Strategy Action Area 3**
  (Digitalisation is one of 5 pillars) and **WEF Global Risks 2025–2026**
  (cyber insecurity is top-10 short- and long-term).
- Aqualia's own operational risks O8 (poor digital deployment) and O9
  (cyberattack response plan deficiency) are catalogued as HIGH — a
  topic they call "low IRO density" is in fact producing HIGH risks.
- **Our read:** Digitalisation is under-scored in the impact
  dimension and under-financialised. Our matrix should reposition it.

### Finding 2 — "Affected communities" (ESRS S3) absent as stand-alone
- Every peer treats S3 (access to water, local development,
  equitable pricing) as material.
- Aqualia folds this into S4 Customer Satisfaction. BUT:
  - Aqualia operates in Colombia where **2024 customer satisfaction
    is 33%** (vs Spain 88%, Czech 90%, France 86%).
  - EU Water Resilience Strategy explicitly addresses affordability.
  - CSRD's ESRS S3 requires separate disclosure of impacts on
    *communities* as distinct from *consumers*.
- **Our read:** S3 is a genuine materiality issue Aqualia has
  collapsed into S4, under-weighting equity/access. Splitting it
  out strengthens both impact and financial materiality (via
  concession renewal risk + affordability regulation).

### Finding 3 — Circular economy (E5) under-weighted
- Aqualia scores E5 with only **3 IROs** — lowest of the E topics.
- **Veolia and Suez** emphasise circular economy as core pillar (Suez
  biofactories; Veolia sludge energy recovery).
- Directive 2024/3019 (recast Urban Wastewater Treatment) mandates
  energy neutrality + circularity. Royal Decree 1985/2024 pushes
  water reuse.
- Aqualia's OWN opportunity OpO4 ("valorise WWTP/desalination
  by-products as competitive lever") implies material upside — but
  only 3 IROs captured.
- **Our read:** E5 is a latent-opportunity topic under-weighted in
  the current matrix.

## 4.  Aqualia genuine differentiation (not "me too")

### Differentiator A — Green finance topic as stand-alone (AQ)
- Aqualia's 2025 review surfaces **"Financial management & green
  finance"** as its own topic (G1, 6 IROs, new in 2025).
- Peers mention sustainable financing but do not generally promote it
  to stand-alone material status.
- **Implication:** Aqualia is ahead on connecting sustainability to
  cost of capital. Our matrix should reward this and our roadmap
  should build on it (green bond pipeline).

### Differentiator B — Integration with Corporate Risk Map
- Aqualia's 2025 DM review explicitly harmonises material topics
  with the Corporate Risk Map (each risk has an owner from the
  Executive Committee). Veolia does this at group level but Aqualia's
  Risk List I/II (codes S1–S2, C1–C5, F1–F3, O1–O14, OpS1–OpO6) is
  an unusually granular public articulation.
- **Implication:** We can reuse Aqualia's own risk codes in our
  matrix — gives judges direct traceability between our findings
  and Aqualia's internal taxonomy. This is the kind of "we did the
  homework" signal consultants live on.

### Differentiator C — TNFD + CSRD integration
- Aqualia explicitly integrates **TNFD** (Taskforce on Nature-related
  Financial Disclosures) into the 2025 double-materiality process.
- Among the Spanish peer set, only Aqualia publicly commits to TNFD
  at this depth.
- **Implication:** E4 (biodiversity) materiality assessment can
  exceed minimum ESRS requirements by leveraging TNFD LEAP
  (Locate, Evaluate, Assess, Prepare) framework.

## 5.  Implications for our short-list

Three-topic short-list should combine **at least one sector-core
topic** (to pass the "me too" test cleanly) with at least one **blind
spot** (to generate the hero finding) and one **differentiator-amplifier**
(to give Aqualia a forward strategy).

|---|---|---|---|

> each player? Is it a 'me too' or a real advantage?"* — two
> differentiators (digitalisation blind spot + green finance edge) plus
> one unavoidable core.
